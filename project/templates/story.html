How do you ask for something from a company, before they have even hired you?

THE PROBLEM

This is the problem some candidates with disabilities face. Imagine trying to convince a company to hire you, whilst simultaneously asking them for a favor.

These "favors" aren't really favors though. They are called accommodations, defined as something a person with a disability might need to complete a task. An American Sign Language interpreter might be an accommodation for a Deaf person. A #2 pencil could be an accomodation for a person who has difficulty typing on a keyboard. Companies are legally required to provide "reasonable" accommodations to candidates during the hiring process and once they are offered a job.
