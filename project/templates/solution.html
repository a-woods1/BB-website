Magic8ball: A system to help recruiters and candidates with disabilities feel more confident about accommodations.

THE PROBLEM

This is the dilemma some candidates with disabilities face. Imagine trying to convince a company to hire you, while simultaneously asking them for a favor.

These "favors" aren't really favors though. They are accommodations. Accommodations can be anything a person with a disability might need to complete a task. An American Sign Language interpreter might be an accommodation for a Deaf person during an in person meeting. A #2 pencil could be an accomodation for a person who has difficulty typing on a keyboard.

The Americans with Disabilities Act requires companies to provide "reasonable" accommodations to candidates during the hiring process. However, asking for an accommodation can be easier said than done, especially because candidates with disabilities feel it could open them up to bias during the hiring process.

This project is a look into the current hiring process for people with disabilities and how to improve the experience of requesting an accommodation, so candidates with disabilities can thrive.
  • Marco is an Engineering recruiter. He posts a new role for a college graduate software engineer. Bloomberg
  • Maria gets a phone interview for the software engineer role! She receives a link to the hiring portal where she can see more information and prepare. Candidate
  • After the phone interview, Marco hears back and Maria has done well. He updates her status to onsite interview. Bloomberg
  • Maria can now log into her portal and see information about her onsite. Based on the information there, she can confidently request two accommodations. Candidate
  • Marco receives Maria’s accommodation requests and is able to quickly and confidently respond to them. Bloomberg
  • Maria gets confirmation from Marco a week before her onsite interview that everything will be in place. Candidate
  • It’s one week later! Marco checks that everything is in place and sends Maria a survey for her to complete about her accommodations experience. Bloomberg
  • After her onsite interview, Maria goes home and sees the survey, she fills it out and share her accommodations experience. Candidate
  • Maria gets an offer and accepts! She logs into her hiring portal to see information about orientation and complete the relevant paperwork. Candidate
